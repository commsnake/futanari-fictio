# The Deep Water Haul: A 3-Book Futanari Story Arc

**Vibe:** Gritty Blue-Collar, High Seas Isolation, Intense Power Exchange.
**Focus:** Futa-on-Male Dynamics, Male Ego Subversion, MPreg (Book 3).
**Core Premise:** "Leo," a young male fresh out of the foster system, needs a cheap transport down the coast to start a new life. He strikes a deal with the crew of a 60ft Tuna Longliner: free passage in exchange for cooking all meals and doing grunt work. The catch? The entire five-person crew are imposing Futanari, and the contract stipulates that any mistakes or disruptions caused by the "Super-Greenhorn" must be "put right" through absolute, unquestioning submission.

## Book 1: The Galley Boy's Debt (The Deckhand & The Deck Boss)

**Focus:** The initial break. Establishing the "put it right" rule.

### 9-Chapter Beats
1. **Chapter 1: Boarding & The Bunk:** Leo boards the vessel. He meets the imposing crew. He is assigned the loud, wet bunk near the engine room and is immediately put to work.
2. **Chapter 2: The First Swell:** Leo struggles to cook in 10-foot swells. He accidentally ruins a meal meant for Deckhand A, spilling hot stew.
3. **Chapter 3: The Contract Enforced:** The Deck Boss intercepts. She reminds Leo of the rule: "Upset the crew, put it right." Leo is dragged to the hold.
4. **Chapter 4: The Reveal:** The Futanari anatomy of Deckhand A and the Deck Boss is revealed. The shock triggers an intense "Authority Gap."
5. **Chapter 5: Primal Release (Deckhand A):** Leo's first punishment. Deckhand A forces him into the "Face-Sit" position on the cold deck of the hold, utilizing her raw muscle and primal dominance.
6. **Chapter 6: The Ongoing Debt:** Days blur. Cooking is exhausting. Deckhand A returns to Leo's bunk repeatedly for "quick, hard oral," establishing that the debt is never truly paid.
7. **Chapter 7: The "Money" Mishap:** During a massive tuna catch, Leo gets in the way of a high-tension line. The Deck Boss is furious and drags him behind a stack of crates.
8. **Chapter 8: Public Exposure (The Deck Boss):** To "put right" the dangerous mistake, the Deck Boss claims him right there on the bloody deck (The Deck Crouch). She highlights his size inferiority and her complete lack of shame.
9. **Chapter 9: The Harbor Approach:** Book 1 ends. Leo realizes he is now the plaything for the two deck workers, who both regularly demand rapid service in the galley pantries or the hold.
