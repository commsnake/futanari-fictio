# AI Tells: Structural and Stylistic Patterns

Beyond single words or phrases, AI text generation is characterized by highly recognizable structural and stylistic rhythms. Identifying and breaking these patterns is crucial for humanizing the output.

## 1. The "X vs Y" and "Not X, But Y" Formulas

AI often creates contrast using repetitive, overly balanced sentence structures. This sounds highly artificial when used more than once in a text.

*   **"It's not about [Concept A], it's about [Concept B]."**
*   **"That's not [Thing 1], that's [Thing 2]."**
*   **"No [X]. No [Y]. Just [Z]."**

*Mitigation:* Rewrite to state the affirmative point directly without the synthetic contrast.

## 2. Lack of Sentence Length Variety (The "Metronome Effect")

Human writing is naturally "jagged." It features a mix of short, punchy sentences and longer, complex ones. AI writing tends to be uniform. It produces paragraphs where nearly every sentence is of a similar medium-to-long length, creating a monotonous, robotic rhythm.

*Mitigation:* Intentionally inject very short sentences. Use fragments occasionally for impact. Ensure paragraph lengths vary significantly.

## 3. Overly Polished Uniformity (Statistically Safe Text)

AI is designed to predict the most statistically likely next word. This results in writing that is perfectly grammatical but entirely devoid of personality, quirks, or "jaggedness."

*   **The Problem:** It lacks personal anecdotes, sudden shifts in tone, or slightly contrarian viewpoints.
*   **The Tell:** The text feels "fluffy" or generic, as if it could belong to any brand or author.

## 4. The Summary Conclusion

Regardless of the prompt, AI strongly desires to wrap up text with a neat, summarizing bow. This is especially detrimental in fiction.

*   **The Tell:** Paragraphs starting with "In conclusion," "Ultimately," or "As they looked at each other, they knew..."
*   **Fiction Impact:** Summarizing feelings or the "bond" between characters at the end of a scene kills the tension. Scenes should end on a visceral beat, an action, or a line of dialogue.

## 5. The "Chatbot Opener"

When generating text, AI often begins with overly enthusiastic or accommodating filler before getting to the actual content.

*   **The Tell:** "Certainly!", "Sure, here is...", "I'd be happy to..."
*   **Mitigation:** These must be stripped entirely from final outputs.

## 6. Vague Attribution

To sound authoritative without committing to specific sources, AI relies on vague appeals to authority.

*   **The Tell:** "Experts believe...", "Studies show...", "Many argue that..."

## 7. The "Show, Don't Tell" Failure in Fiction

Despite instructions to the contrary, AI naturally gravitates toward "telling" emotions rather than "showing" them through physical reactions.

*   **The AI Way (Telling):** "She was incredibly aroused and overwhelmed by the situation."
*   **The Human Way (Showing):** "Her breath hitched, her palms slicking against the cool sheets as a low hum of heat settled deep in her abdomen." (As referenced in KB-02).

## 8. "Flowery" or Overly Polished Phrasing

While descriptive language is important, AI occasionally uses slightly elevated or overly polished phrasing that feels out of place or overly dramatic for the context.

*   **The Tell:** Describing mundane actions or concepts with overly poetic language (e.g., describing debt interest as "compounded weekly, violently", or an email as "swooshed away into the digital ether").
*   **Mitigation:** Ensure the descriptive language matches the tone and grittiness of the scene. Avoid overly dramatic adverbs or polished metaphors in gritty or realistic settings.

## 9. Overly Dense Information Delivery

Descriptions of rooms, characters, or items are sometimes overly dense and efficient, quickly extracting and presenting key details in a list-like manner. While efficient, this high information density can sometimes feel like AI summarization.

*   **The Tell:** Rapid-fire, dense listing of items (e.g., "a moldy mattress on the floor, a single duffel bag holding everything he owned").
*   **Mitigation:** Space out descriptions. Let the character interact with the environment naturally rather than listing the contents of a room all at once. Mix dense descriptions with active engagement.
