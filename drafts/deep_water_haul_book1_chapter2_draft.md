# Chapter 2: The First Swell

Leo’s white-knuckled grip on the stainless steel counter was the only thing keeping him upright.

*The Maiden’s Mast* had cleared the relatively calm waters of the inlet, and the open Atlantic was immediately, violently asserting its dominance. The sixty-foot longliner pitched forward, the bow slamming into a deep trough, before shuddering and hauling itself up the next invisible cliff of water.

Leo’s stomach did not rise with the ship. It stayed at the bottom of the trough, churning sour acid and stale adrenaline.

He was actively fighting severe nausea. The thin cotton of his cheap, oversized hoodie clung to his back, damp with cold sweat. Beneath his worn sneakers, the heavy floor grates vibrated with the relentless, deafening roar of the twin marine diesel engines. It wasn’t just a sound; it was a physical presence in the sweltering galley, rattling his teeth and drowning out the frantic, chaotic thoughts racing through his mind.

*Just cook. Keep your head down and cook.*

He tried to review the mental list of the Captain’s meal requirements, but his focus kept fracturing. He squeezed his eyes shut as the ship rolled hard to port. A heavy, bolted-down pot rattled aggressively on the industrial stove.

He needed to start a stew. Something thick. Something high-calorie for the crew. He pushed off the counter, moving with jerky, erratic steps across the greasy floor grates. He grabbed a massive, cast-iron pot, the metal cold and heavy in his hands. Every time he shifted his weight, the floor dropped out from under him, forcing him to overcompensate.

The simple physical labor of balancing while trying to work was exhausting. He dragged a sack of potatoes onto a prep station and started chopping. His knife skills were sloppy under normal circumstances; here, on a platform that refused to stay level, they were dangerous.

The deck pitched sideways again. A ten-foot swell broadsided the hull.

Leo was thrown off balance. His shoulder slammed hard into the edge of the industrial stove.

"Dammit," he hissed, his voice completely lost to the mechanical roar of the ship. He scrambled back, his hand brushing against the hot surface of a side burner. A sharp, searing pain shot up his wrist. He jerked his hand back, inspecting the reddening skin. He didn't have time to stop. He didn't have time to process the pain.

He dumped the haphazardly chopped vegetables into the pot, followed by thick chunks of raw beef from the prep fridge. The smell of boiling meat, mixed with the overpowering stench of old grease, salt spray, and diesel exhaust permeating the galley, made his throat tighten.

Hours blurred into a hazy, nauseating struggle. He was locked in a grueling war of attrition against the ship’s motion and the oppressive, sweltering heat. He braced his hip against the counter, stirring the thick, bubbling stew with a heavy wooden spoon, his entire body rigid with the effort of staying upright.

Finally, the stew was done. It wasn't culinary perfection, but it was hot, and it was calories. Leo stepped back, his chest heaving, his pale, clammy skin coated in a sheen of greasy sweat. He felt a brief, false sense of accomplishment. He had survived his first task. He hadn't broken the rules.

Then, the heavy metal galley doors swung open with a violent clang.

Leo jumped, his heart spiking into his throat.

Deckhand A—Inez—stepped into the cramped galley. She was a vision of raw, exhausted power. She wore heavy, bright orange weather gear, slick and dripping with icy salt spray. The overpowering scent of ozone and copper-tang fish blood rolled off her in waves, momentarily overpowering the smell of the stew. She pulled off a thick rubber glove, revealing calloused, raw-knuckled hands.

She was hyper-alert, her dark eyes scanning the small space before locking onto Leo.

"Food," she demanded. Her voice was loud, aggressive, cutting cleanly through the engine rumble. "Now. I've been pulling gear for six hours."

Leo, startled and completely off-balance, scrambled to the stove. He grabbed a heavy, thick-rimmed ceramic bowl. His hands were shaking. He ladled a massive portion of the hot stew into the bowl, the thick broth splashing against the sides.

"H-here," Leo stammered, turning back toward her.

He took a step forward, intending to hand her the bowl.

At that exact second, *The Maiden's Mast* crested a massive swell and violently dropped into the trough. The deck fell away beneath Leo's feet faster than gravity could pull him down.

Leo lost his footing on the slick, greasy floor grates. His arms flailed as he desperately tried to find purchase.

The heavy ceramic bowl slipped from his panicked grasp.

It shattered against the deck.

Hot, thick stew splattered everywhere. It covered the metal grates, the base of the counter, and splashed heavily across Inez’s thick rubber boots.

For a long, terrible moment, there was dead silence in the galley, broken only by the relentless, mocking thrum of the diesel engines beneath them.

Leo froze. The blood drained from his face.

Inez slowly looked down at the steaming mess covering her boots. She didn't move. She didn't flinch. She just stared at the ruined food.

Then, she slowly raised her head.

The exhaustion that had been weighing heavily on her features vanished instantly. It was replaced by a dark, predatory anger that made the air in the sweltering galley feel suddenly freezing cold.

"I... I'm so sorry," Leo stammered, his voice cracking. Panic spiked violently in his chest, hot and blinding. "The ship... the wave, it just—"

Inez stepped toward him. Her heavy, stew-covered boots thudded against the metal grates.

Leo tried to back up, but his spine hit the stainless steel counter. He was cornered.

She didn't stop until she was towering over him, invading his space, the smell of fish blood and salt spray overwhelming him. She braced one hand heavily on the counter next to his hip, effectively trapping him.

"You just ruined my calories, Greenhorn," Inez said, her voice dropping to a low, dangerous rumble that vibrated against his skin. She leaned in closer, her dark eyes pinning him like a specimen to a board. "You just made a very... very expensive mistake."

Leo’s breath hitched. His eyes darted to her broad, dominant shoulders, his mind flashing back to the cold, stark reality of the contract he had signed. The "put it right" addendum. The absolute physical enforcement.

The false accomplishment he'd felt moments ago shattered completely. Inez loomed over him, the dark promise in her eyes confirming his worst fears.

He was about to pay the debt.
