# KB-10: Spatial Blocking System (Kinematic Scene Blocking Matrix)

To ensure the "Anti-Pretzel Protocol" (as outlined in KB-02) is strictly followed, the writing system utilizes the **Kinematic Scene Blocking Matrix (KSBM)**.

This matrix acts as a text-based reference grid that tracks limb placement, orientation, and contact points throughout a scene, particularly during high-action or explicit sequences (Phases 4-8 of the Erotic Cycle).

## 1. The Core Principle
Physical descriptions must respect human (and modified Futanari) anatomy. A character cannot perform an action if their limbs are currently occupied or physically unable to reach the target due to positioning, leverage, or gravity.

## 2. Using the KSBM in Prompts

When injecting complex spatial instructions into a chapter prompt, authors/systems must include a brief KSBM state summary to ground the AI before it generates prose.

### Standard KSBM Syntax

The syntax defines the state of the Anchor (A) and Seeker (S):

*   **[A: Posture/Location]**: (e.g., Standing, Kneeling, Pinned against Wall)
*   **[A: Hands]**: (e.g., Left gripping S's wrist, Right holding S's waist)
*   **[A: Legs]**: (e.g., Braced wide on the floor)
*   **[S: Posture/Location]**: (e.g., Bent over, Kneeling, Straddling)
*   **[S: Hands]**: (e.g., Gripping the headboard, Braced on the mattress)
*   **[S: Legs]**: (e.g., Spread wide, Knees on the floor)
*   **[Point of Contact]**: (e.g., Penetration point, chest-to-back pressure)

## 3. Transition Rules

When characters change positions, the prose *must* narrate the transition. The system cannot simply jump from Missionary to Doggy Style without describing the shift in limbs, breaking of contact, and re-establishment of the new position.

*   **Rule of Leverage:** Emphasize *how* the characters move. Who is bearing the weight? How are the massive anatomic differences (specifically in Futanari dynamics) physically accommodated during the turn?
*   **Breaking Contact:** Clearly state when a hand lets go of a grip or when bodies separate before re-engaging.
