# KB-08: Amazon KDP Compliance Protocol (Dark Erotica & CNC)

This module outlines the mandatory guidelines for writing Dark Erotica, Intense Power Exchange, and Consensual Non-Consent (CNC) scenarios to ensure compliance with Amazon Kindle Direct Publishing (KDP) Content Guidelines.

KDP algorithms and human reviewers strictly prohibit actual non-consent, abuse, and certain taboos. To maintain a gritty, dark tone without triggering a ban, the narrative must clearly framework all "forced" actions within absolute, unambiguous consent.

## 1. The Pre-Consent Framework (The "Contract")
Actual non-consent is banned. Consensual Non-Consent (CNC) is allowed if the consent is established *before* the acts occur.
- **Implementation:** Characters must explicitly agree to the power dynamic or rules of engagement early in the narrative.
- **Narrative Example:** Signing a draconian contract, taking a specific vow, or explicitly stating "I agree to submit to whatever you decide" before an encounter begins.

## 2. Unambiguous Safewords
A safeword or safe gesture is the ultimate proof of consent in high-intensity scenes.
- **Implementation:** Establish a safeword (e.g., "Red") or a physical safe gesture (if verbal communication is impossible, like in a noisy engine room) prior to any intense power exchange.
- **Usage:** The tension in the scene must derive from the protagonist *choosing not to use* the safeword, despite the intensity. This confirms ongoing consent.

## 3. Internal Monologue as the Consent Engine ("The Turn")
In scenes where dialogue is minimal or the submissive character is physically overwhelmed, internal monologue must explicitly confirm desire.
- **Implementation:** Use "The Turn" mechanic. The scene begins with resistance or feeling overwhelmed, but at the peak of tension, the character's internal thoughts must explicitly confirm arousal and a desire to be overpowered.
- **Banned Approach:** The character internally begging for it to stop while being forced.
- **Required Approach:** "He panicked at the sheer force of her, but then, the dark heat bloomed in his chest. *He wanted this. He wanted to be crushed.*"

## 4. Mandatory Aftercare (The KDP Shield)
Aftercare demonstrates to reviewers that the dominant behavior is part of an agreed-upon erotic dynamic, not actual abuse.
- **Implementation:** Every high-intensity or CNC scene must conclude with the dominant character providing care.
- **Actions:** Physical cleaning, providing water, grounding touch (e.g., rubbing the back of the neck), checking in emotionally, or affirming the submissive's safety.

## 5. Age and Agency
- All characters involved in sexual situations must be unambiguously presented as consenting adults (18+).
- Avoid any ambiguity regarding age.
- Characters must possess the agency to consent (they cannot be fundamentally incapacitated by drugs/alcohol during the initiation of the act, though dub-con elements via fantasy magic/sci-fi tropes require careful maneuvering, usually relying on the "Internal Monologue" rule above to confirm desire).

## 6. Content Warnings (Front Matter)
- While KDP itself does not have a formal "trigger warning" feature, responsibly placing a content warning page at the beginning of the book sets expectations for the reader and provides a layer of professional defense.
- Example: "This dark romance explores themes of intense power exchange and Consensual Non-Consent (CNC). All characters are consenting adults."
