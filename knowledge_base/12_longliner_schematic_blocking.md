# Kinematic Scene Blocking System: 60' Longliner Schematic ("The Maiden's Mast")

To effectively map the Spatial Blocking System (Anti-Pretzel Protocol) within the "Deep Water Haul" arc, use this spatial layout of the 60' F-Class Longliner (*The Maiden's Mast*). This serves as the environmental grid for calculating character positions, movement, and physical collisions during explicit or high-action scenes.

## Vessel Layout (Fore to Aft)

### 1. Foredeck & Foc'sle (Lower Deck)
*   **Dimensions:** ~15' L x 12' W (tapering to bow).
*   **Layout:**
    *   **V-Berth (Crew Quarters):** Lower level. 6 stacked bunks (3 per side), reinforced steel framing ("F-Designation"), narrow central aisle (~3 feet wide).
    *   **Atmosphere:** Cramped, low ceiling (6'2" clearance), dim lighting.
    *   **Blocking Constraints:** Minimal standing room. Intimacy here forces characters onto bunks (limited horizontal space, high vertical restriction). The narrow aisle means passing bodies must press together.

### 2. Main Deck (Midships & Galley/Head)
*   **Dimensions:** ~20' L x 18' W (full breadth).
*   **Layout:**
    *   **Galley (Port Side):** Full deck-level. Extended dry storage ("F-Designation"), prep counters, small mess table with bolted-down bench seating for 4-5.
    *   **Head/Washroom (Starboard Side):** Single shower unit (high-pressure), marine toilet, cramped sink area.
    *   **Companionways:** Stairs leading down forward to the V-Berth, and up to the Wheelhouse. Aft door leads to the open working deck.
    *   **Blocking Constraints:** The galley is a prime location for Leo's tasks. Counters provide hip-height bracing points. The head is extremely tight—two bodies inside requires complete skin-to-skin compression against bulkheads.

### 3. Wheelhouse (Upper Deck / Raised)
*   **Dimensions:** ~10' L x 12' W.
*   **Layout:**
    *   **Helm Station:** Forward-facing controls, radar, sonar displays.
    *   **Captain's Quarters:** Aft of the helm. Single berth, chart table, secure comms.
    *   **Blocking Constraints:** Elevated vantage point looking down at the working deck. Tight but distinct sections. The Captain's berth offers privacy and slightly more space than the V-Berth, functioning as an interrogation/punishment zone.

### 4. Working Deck (Aft) & Hold
*   **Dimensions:** ~25' L x 18' W.
*   **Layout:**
    *   **Open Deck:** Exposed to the elements. Wet, slippery, constantly moving.
    *   **Fish Hold Hatch:** Centerline access to the 28,000 lbs hold (directly beneath).
    *   **Gear Stations:** 50-mile LP mono longline shooter (stern), 8,000-bait live wells, 2.5-ton ice maker & bait freezer (port/starboard bulkheads).
    *   **Engine Room Access:** Hatch leading down to the 425HP Cummins and 50KW John Deere Generator.
    *   **Blocking Constraints:** Environmental hazards (rolling deck, cold spray). Requires characters to maintain three points of contact. Sex scenes here are brutal, exposed, and rely on pinning against bulkheads, bait freezers, or the gunwales.

## Blocking Nodes & Environmental Props

When injecting the Kinematic Scene Blocking Matrix (KSBM) into a prompt set on the vessel, use these reference nodes:

*   **[Node: V-Berth Aisle]** - Prop: Reinforced bunk ladder. Risk: Head strikes on low ceiling.
*   **[Node: Galley Counter]** - Prop: Bolted mess table, prep counter edge (36" high). Ideal for bent-over positioning.
*   **[Node: Shower Stall]** - Prop: High-pressure nozzle, slick fiberglass walls. Zero clearance.
*   **[Node: Captain's Chart Table]** - Prop: Flat, waist-high surface. Elevated authority.
*   **[Node: Bait Freezer Bulkhead]** - Prop: Cold, humming steel wall. Slippery deck below. Pinning/trapping mechanics.
*   **[Node: Engine Room Ladder]** - Prop: Greasy metal rungs, deafening noise (425HP). Forced vertical positioning.
