## 5. Plot Beats (20–25 beats)
1. Leo stands on the rain-slicked dock, looking up at the imposing hull of the 60ft Tuna Longliner.
2. He hesitates, clutching his meager duffel bag, feeling the desperation of having no other options.
3. Leo climbs the gangway, his boots slipping slightly on the wet metal.
4. He steps onto the main deck, immediately assaulted by the smell of diesel, salt, and old fish.
5. The First Mate intercepts him—a tall, heavily muscled woman with a stern expression.
6. She demands to see his "Articles of Employment" contract to verify he understands the deal.
7. Leo hands over the folded, damp paper, confirming he trades free passage for grunt work.
8. The First Mate visually assesses him, noting his lean, inexperienced build.
9. She introduces him to the rest of the crew (Deck Boss, Deckhand A, Engineer) who are equally physically intimidating.
10. The crew surrounds him briefly, the physical size gap making Leo feel boxed in and small.
11. The First Mate pulls out a laminated sheet outlining the vessel's strict rules.
12. She reads the "put right" clause aloud: any disruption caused by the greenhorn means absolute, unquestioning physical submission.
13. To satisfy KDP compliance, she establishes the mandatory safeword protocol.
14. Due to the engine noise, she teaches him a physical safeword (a specific, sharp double-tap).
15. Leo must demonstrate the safeword to prove he understands.
16. The First Mate leads him below deck, down a narrow, steep ladder well.
17. The air becomes hotter, thicker, and louder as they descend.
18. She shows him his bunk: a cramped, damp space right against the engine room bulkhead.
19. The noise of the idling engine is a constant, low vibration through the thin mattress.
20. He drops his bag on the bunk, realizing the lack of privacy.
21. The First Mate doesn't let him rest; she immediately hands him a bucket and a scrub brush.
22. She orders him back topside to clean the galley before they even leave the harbor.
23. Leo climbs back up, the reality of his situation sinking in.
24. He begins scrubbing the galley floor as the ship's horn sounds.
25. The longliner pulls away from the dock, severing Leo's ties to the mainland.
