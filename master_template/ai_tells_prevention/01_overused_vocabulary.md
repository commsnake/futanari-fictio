# AI Tells: Overused Vocabulary Blacklist

This document serves as an exhaustive reference for words and phrases that are highly indicative of AI-generated text. When these terms appear, especially in clusters or outside of highly technical contexts, they break immersion and flag the writing as artificial.

## High-Alarm Single Words (The "Delve" List)

The following words should be strictly avoided or used with extreme caution. AI models rely on these terms as cruthes to sound authoritative, sophisticated, or polished.

*   **Tapestry** (e.g., "a rich tapestry of...")
*   **Realm** (e.g., "in the realm of...")
*   **Delve** (e.g., "let's delve into...")
*   **Nuanced** / **Nuance**
*   **Vibrant**
*   **Dynamic**
*   **Compelling**
*   **Testament** (e.g., "a testament to...")
*   **Overarching**
*   **Nestled**
*   **Robust**
*   **Seamless** / **Seamlessly**
*   **Crucial**
*   **Pivotal**
*   **Elevate** (e.g., "elevate your experience")
*   **Navigate** (e.g., "navigate the complexities")
*   **Foster** (e.g., "foster a sense of...")
*   **Showcase**
*   **Ensure**
*   **Embark**
*   **Unwavering**
*   **Resonate**
*   **Multifaceted**
*   **Symbiotic**

## Generic Transition and Filler Phrases

AI often uses these phrases to link ideas together or open/close paragraphs. They add no real substance and bloat the word count.

*   **"In today's fast-paced world..."**
*   **"It is essential to..."** / **"It's essential to..."**
*   **"It's worth noting that..."**
*   **"In conclusion..."**
*   **"Ultimately..."**
*   **"As a reminder..."**
*   **"Furthermore..."** / **"Moreover..."** (when overused)
*   **"Firstly..."** / **"Secondly..."**
*   **"There are a few considerations..."**

## Significance Inflation

AI tends to overstate the importance of standard actions or events, making the prose feel unnecessarily grandiose.

*   **"A game changer"**
*   **"A watershed moment"**
*   **"Unlocks value"**
*   **"Drives impact"**
*   **"Cutting-edge"**
*   **"Future-ready"**

## Copula Avoidance and "Feature" Words

Instead of using simple verbs (e.g., "is", "has"), AI often substitutes them with overly formal equivalents.

*   **"Serves as..."** (instead of "is")
*   **"Featuring..."**
*   **"Boasting..."**

## Erotica/Fiction-Specific AI Tells

Based on the knowledge base (KB-02), these specific cliches and summaries kill narrative tension in fictional contexts.

*   **"Oh baby, so good, more please, take it all, hot and heavy."** (Porn-Script Clichés)
*   **"Combined bond..."** (Summary Endings)
*   Summarizing the character's feelings at the end of a scene instead of ending on a visceral action beat.
