# Chapter 4: The Reveal in the Hold

Leo was shivering so violently his teeth clicked together. The air inside the F-Class fish hold was a biting, aggressive cold that seemed to suck the heat directly from his marrow. He huddled against the heavy plastic fish crates, pulling his knees tight to his chest. The harsh, industrial fluorescent lighting buzzed angrily overhead, casting stark, unforgiving shadows across the frost-covered steel of the bulkheads.

He looked up at the two women looming over him.

Ox, the massive Deck Boss, stood with her arms crossed over her broad chest. Her face was an impassive wall of authority. Next to her, Inez breathed heavily, the adrenaline of the galley confrontation still burning in her dark eyes.

"You signed the Articles of Employment," Ox said, her low rumble echoing off the icy metal. "You read the clause. 'Any disruption to the vessel’s operation, including the waste of provisions, will be put right through absolute, unquestioning service to the aggrieved party.' Do you remember that, Super-Greenhorn?"

Leo swallowed the dry lump of terror in his throat. He remembered the heavy black ink. He remembered skimming it, blinded by the fifteen-thousand-dollar gang debt waiting for him on the mainland. He had thought 'service' meant scrubbing the decks or cleaning the bilges.

"I..." Leo stammered, his breath pluming in the freezing air. "I'll do whatever you want. I'll clean the whole ship. I'll take double watches."

Inez let out a sharp, derisive scoff. She stepped forward, the heavy rubber soles of her boots crunching on the frosted grates. She stopped just inches from where Leo huddled on the floor.

"I don't want the ship clean," Inez growled, looking down at him with a dark, predatory hunger. "I want my compensation." She pointed a thick finger at the icy floor directly in front of her boots. "Get on your knees, boy."

Leo froze. His street-kid pride, the hard, calloused shell that had kept him alive on the streets of Newark, flared up instinctively. He pressed his back harder against the crates, shaking his head slightly. He wouldn't do it. He couldn't.

Ox saw the hesitation. The Deck Boss didn't say a word. She simply stepped forward, reaching out with a hand the size of a dinner plate. She clamped it onto Leo’s shoulder.

The physical weight of her grip was staggering. She didn't shove him; she simply leaned into it, applying an unbearable downward pressure.

Leo’s resistance crumbled instantly. His knees hit the freezing metal grates with a sharp *crack*. A jolt of pain shot up his thighs, but he didn't dare move. He was kneeling, perfectly positioned at Inez's boots, completely overpowered.

"Good," Inez breathed, the anger in her voice shifting into something much darker and heavier.

Leo kept his eyes glued to the floor, his heart hammering a frantic, bird-like rhythm against his ribs. He waited for the blow. He waited for the kick.

Instead, he heard the heavy, metallic *clack* of thick brass clasps being undone.

He slowly looked up.

Inez was unfastening the heavy suspenders of her foul-weather overalls. The thick, yellow canvas dropped to the floor, pooling heavily around her boots. Underneath, she wore only a pair of dark, sweat-stained thermal boxers.

She hooked her thumbs into the waistband and pushed them down.

The breath completely vanished from Leo’s lungs. His mind blanked, a sudden, violent short-circuit in his brain as it tried to process the impossible visual information in front of him.

Inez Rivera was Futanari.

The sheer scale of the women. It all slammed into him at once. He had been so desperate to escape the mainland that he had completely ignored every warning sign.

He was trapped on a sixty-foot longliner with an all-Futanari crew.

"Like what you see, greenhorn?" Inez asked, her voice a low, gravelly purr. She took a half-step forward, forcing Leo to lean his head back to avoid contact. The heat radiating from her was intense, a stark contrast to the biting cold of the room.

Before Leo could form a thought, a second metallic *clack* echoed in the hold.

He dragged his paralyzed gaze to the side.

Ox was unfastening her own heavy canvas gear. The Deck Boss let the thick material fall away, exposing herself to the freezing air.

If Inez was terrifying, Ox was a force of nature.

He was not just smaller and weaker; he was entirely outmatched in every conceivable metric. The power structure wasn't just uneven; it was an absolute monopoly.

"The contract demands service," Ox rumbled, her voice vibrating through the soles of Leo's shoes. "You owe the deckhand a meal, boy. Now, you provide one."

Inez stepped closer. Her thick thighs brushed against Leo's trembling shoulders. She reached down, her large hand grabbing a handful of his messy hair. She didn't pull, but the threat was explicit.

"Open up," Inez demanded, her voice dropping into a dark, demanding register.

He thought about fighting, but the memory of Ox lifting him by the collar with one hand killed the thought instantly.

He thought about the safeword. The double-tap to the dominant shoulder. Morgan had made him practice it. *Tap, tap.*

The realization settled deep in his gut, cold and heavy as a stone. There was nowhere to run. There was no escape. The only way out of this room was through.

Ox's massive hand settled heavily on his other shoulder, a stark reminder of the inescapable physical reality. "Begin," she commanded.

Leo closed his eyes. The shivering in his chest was violent, a mix of freezing cold and absolute terror. Slowly, agonizingly, he raised his trembling hands. He didn't reach for the safeword.
