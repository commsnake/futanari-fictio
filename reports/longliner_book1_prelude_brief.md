## 4. Plot Summary (Verbatim)
Leo is sitting on the floor with his back to the door in a seedy boarding house, holding off the world while an argument and shouting occur just outside. He is desperately flicking between the original job ad on his phone and the digital copy of the contract he has been sent, unable to believe the brutal clauses inside.

## 5. Plot Beats (20–25 beats)
1. Leo is pressed hard against the flimsy, peeling door of a seedy boarding house room.
2. Violent shouting and the sound of heavy boots echo from the hallway outside.
3. He holds his breath, his knuckles white against the floorboards, waiting for the doorknob to turn.
4. The argument outside is about money; it’s the mainland gang tracking his $15,000 debt.
5. The footsteps eventually move past his door and fade down the hall.
6. Leo exhales, slumping slightly but remaining braced against the door, essentially holding off the world.
7. He pulls out his cracked smartphone, the only source of light in the dim room.
8. He opens the original job posting: an ad for a Galley Boy on an Astor Longliner, departing Barnegat Light.
9. The ad highlights the massive, desperate need for crew and an abnormally high, immediate sign-on cash bonus.
10. He flicks his thumb across the screen, switching to the PDF attachment he was sent in response to his application.
11. The document is the "Articles of Employment: F-Class Longliner."
12. He scrolls past the standard labor clauses, his eyes catching on Section IV: The "Put it Right" Addendum.
13. He reads the words "Consensual Non-Consent," "Free Use Agreement," and "Physical Enforcement."
14. Disbelief washes over him. He whispers, "They surely can't mean..."
15. He re-reads the clauses, convinced it must be a sick joke or some archaic maritime hazing ritual.
16. He looks at the "Visual Safeword" section, the sheer absurdity of needing a hand gesture instead of a verbal stop word sinking in.
17. Another loud crash from down the hall jerks his attention away.
18. He checks his bank balance on his phone—completely empty. The gang debt is a death sentence.
19. He looks back at the massive sign-on bonus promised upon signing the contract.
20. His foster-kid survival instincts kick in: he rationalizes that he can sign it, take the cash to pay off the gang or buy a bus ticket far away, and simply jump ship before they depart.
21. He convinces himself that no one actually enforces "Free Use" contracts in the real world.
22. With a trembling finger, he types his digital signature onto the document.
23. He hits 'Send' to reply to the Captain.
24. He begins frantically shoving his meager belongings into a single duffel bag.
25. The prelude ends with Leo slipping out the window onto a fire escape, the signed contract sitting in his sent folder like a ticking time bomb.

## 6. Scene Function
* Establish Leo's core motivation and the pressing danger of the gang debt.
* Reveal the "Articles of Employment" contract to the reader *before* Leo boards, highlighting his willful ignorance of its severity.
* Build tension leading into Chapter 1.

* **What threatens to break or transform:** His safety. By choosing the contract, he trades an immediate physical threat for a long-term psychological and physical trap, though he doesn't realize it yet.
