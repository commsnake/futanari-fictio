## 1. Chapter Label
Book 1, Chapter 9: The Harbor Approach

## 2. POV (Point of View)
* **POV character:** Leo
* **POV style:** 3rd Limited
* **Justification:** Essential to show Leo's internal resolution and his final realization that he does not want to leave the ship.

## 3. Genre & Subgenre
Gritty Blue-Collar Erotica • Dark Futanari

## 4. Plot Summary (Verbatim)
Book 1 ends as they approach the first port for offloading. Leo has paid his physical debts to the deck crew, but he realizes he doesn't want to get off the boat. The isolation of the sea has become his new, twisted sanctuary.

## 5. Plot Beats (20–25 beats)
1. Several days have passed since the claiming in the bunk.
2. The *Maiden's Mast* is heavy in the water, her hold full of the massive tuna catch.
3. The ocean swells calm significantly as they approach the coastal shelf.
4. Leo is in the galley, moving with newfound confidence and balance.
5. He prepares a final, massive celebratory meal for the crew before they hit port.
6. The crew enters the galley, exhausted but highly satisfied with the haul.
7. Ox gives Leo a heavy, approving nod as she takes her plate. The dynamic is settled.
8. Inez jokes roughly with Leo, treating him as a recognized, albeit bottom-tier, member of the crew.
9. After the meal, Leo heads above deck to clean the remaining gear.
10. He sees the faint outline of the mainland on the horizon—the first land he's seen in weeks.
11. Instead of relief, the sight of the coast fills him with deep dread.
12. The mainland represents the gang debt, the foster system, and his old, terrifying independence.
13. He realizes the isolation of the ship is the only place he feels safe.
14. He finishes cleaning the deck, watching the coastline grow closer.
15. Morgan Thorne (First Mate) approaches him on deck, leaning against the railing.
16. She coldly informs him that he's earned his $10k sign-on bonus and can pack his bags.
17. She expects him to run, just like every other greenhorn they've hired.
18. Leo hesitates, looking at the approaching harbor lights.
19. He looks back at Morgan, realizing she is the next level of authority he must navigate.
20. Leo explicitly tells Morgan he isn't getting off the boat. He wants to sign on for the next trip.
21. Morgan raises an eyebrow, genuinely surprised, her cold demeanor cracking slightly.
22. She warns him that the next trip is into deeper water, and the rules only get harder.
23. Leo nods, accepting the terms without hesitation. He accepts his submission fully.
24. Morgan smirks, a dark, assessing look in her eyes, accepting his decision.
25. The chapter ends with the ship docking, Leo standing firmly on the deck, looking out at the mainland he has actively chosen to abandon.
