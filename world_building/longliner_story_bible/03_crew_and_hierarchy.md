# 03. Crew & Hierarchy

The vessel operates under a strict, unforgiving hierarchy. The isolation of the open ocean amplifies this dynamic, creating a closed ecosystem where the Captain's word is absolute law, and rank dictates every interaction. The entire active crew is Futanari, representing a cross-section of the 0.05% demographic who have gravitated toward elite, isolated blue-collar work.

## The Hierarchy

### 1. The Captain ("The Old Man")
- **Role:** Absolute Authority. She dictates the route, the catch quotas, and the ultimate fate of anyone on board.
- **Background/Vibe:** Older, weathered, perhaps of New England or maritime Canadian descent. She speaks rarely but with finality.
- **Narrative Arc:** She is the overarching force. In Books 1 and 2, she is an intimidating background presence. In Book 3, she reveals her ultimate intention to use Leo for legacy/breeding (MPG-01).

### 2. The First Mate
- **Role:** Second-in-Command, Operations. She translates the Captain's orders into reality and manages the ship's navigation and overall discipline.
- **Background/Vibe:** Sharp, calculating, perhaps of West Coast/Pacific Northwest logging or fishing background. She uses psychological pressure as much as physical intimidation.
- **Narrative Arc:** Takes direct supervision of Leo in Book 2, utilizing "Emotional Shielding" during the storm before asserting a brutal, possessive claim.

### 3. The Engineer
- **Role:** Technical Specialist. She rules the engine room, a sweltering, deafening environment critical to the ship's survival.
- **Background/Vibe:** Grumpy, fiercely protective of her machinery, covered in grease. Could be of Midwestern industrial or Rust Belt descent.
- **Narrative Arc:** Features heavily in Book 2. She uses the cramped, dangerous environment of the engine room to enforce the "put it right" rule via the "Wall Pin" position.

### 4. The Deck Boss
- **Role:** Lead Laborer. She oversees the physical extraction of the catch, managing the deckhands and the brutal, freezing work on the main deck.
- **Background/Vibe:** Massive, scarred, hyper-aggressive. Perhaps of Gulf Coast or Southern brawler background. She is the physical enforcer of the ship.
- **Narrative Arc:** The primary antagonist/dominator in Book 1. She enforces Leo's first major physical debts and claims him via adapted missionary positions.

### 5. Deckhand A
- **Role:** "The Muscle." The lowest-ranking Futanari on the vessel, but still infinitely more powerful than the MMC. She executes the heavy lifting on the deck.
- **Background/Vibe:** Younger, eager to prove herself to the Deck Boss, perhaps an immigrant or first-generation roughneck.
- **Narrative Arc:** The first to collect on Leo's "debt" in Book 1 (SER-05 trope), triggering his internal realization (The Turn) that he is aroused by the submission.

## The "Super-Greenhorn" (The MMC)

### Leo
- **Role:** Galley Boy / Grunt Labor. He is at the absolute bottom of the hierarchy, existing entirely outside the Futanari power dynamic until he is forcefully integrated.
- **Background:** Fresh out of the foster system, broke, desperate, and used to running away.
- **Status:** He signs the "Articles of Employment," explicitly agreeing to the CNC "put it right" rules in exchange for transport down the coast. He intends to jump ship at the first major port, but the combination of extreme isolation, grueling labor, and intense physical domination breaks his ego, making the ship his twisted sanctuary.
