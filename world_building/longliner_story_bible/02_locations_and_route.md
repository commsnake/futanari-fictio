# 02. Locations & Route

The story takes place along a gritty, blue-collar American East Coast commercial fishing route. The isolated nature of the open sea serves as a critical narrative pressure cooker.

## The Mid-Atlantic Run

The longliner's primary route focuses on the Mid-Atlantic states, operating out of historically significant commercial fishing ports.

### 1. Boarding Port: Barnegat Light, New Jersey
- **Location Context:** Located at the northern tip of Long Beach Island. It is a primary commercial seaport that relies heavily on commercial fishing year-round.
- **Vibe:** Industrial, cold, pragmatic. It smells of diesel and old salt.
- **Narrative Purpose:** This is where Leo desperately signs his contract and boards the vessel. It's home to many long-line fishing boats and scallopers, making it the perfect setting for him to find this specific ship.

### 2. Mid-Route Port: Belford, New Jersey
- **Location Context:** Located in the shelter of Sandy Hook on the northern-most section of the New Jersey coast.
- **Vibe:** Busy, efficient, surrounded by processing plants and ready highway access to New York.
- **Narrative Purpose:** This serves as the offloading point at the end of Book 1. The port supports a fleet of long-liners and seiners. The proximity to the fish processing plants means the crew is hyper-focused on offloading quickly, creating a high-stress environment where Leo realizes he doesn't want to leave the strange sanctuary of the boat.

### 3. Intended Disembarkation: Point Pleasant or Atlantic City, New Jersey
- **Location Context:** Major commercial fishing hubs further along the coast. Atlantic City has a fleet operating in the shadow of the casinos, while Point Pleasant is a locus for shellfisheries.
- **Narrative Purpose:** Leo's original plan was simply to get transport down the coast, perhaps to disappear into the chaotic environment of Atlantic City or find grunt work in Point Pleasant. However, as the arc progresses, the idea of disembarking at these locations fades; he chooses to remain bound to the crew and the isolated life at sea.
