# 11. Timeline & Sea Conditions

This document outlines the overarching timeline for "The Deep Water Haul" arc, tracking elapsed time, geographical locations, and average sea conditions. The isolated, hazardous environment of the Mid-Atlantic commercial fishing routes serves as the core narrative pressure cooker for the crew and the protagonist, Leo.

## Overarching 3-Book Arc

The story progresses in a linear fashion, spanning roughly two months of grueling deep-sea commercial fishing operations. It starts with shorter, coastal-adjacent runs before extending into dangerous multi-week expeditions.
- **Book 1:** A short initial run. Leo boards and gets initiated. Ends with offloading.
- **Book 2:** A 10-day intermediate trip. Increased isolation, severe weather event.
- **Book 3:** A multi-week long expedition. Deep water, final structural shifts, mechanical failure, and conclusion.

---

## Book 1 Breakdown: The Galley Boy's Debt

**Duration:** A short, initial offshore run (approximately 4-6 days).
**Start Location:** Barnegat Light, NJ (Boarding).
**End Location:** Belford, NJ (Mid-Route Port Offload).

| Chapter | Location & Narrative Focus | Sea Conditions & Environment |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **1-4** | **Barnegat Light, NJ.** Docked. Leo signs the contract, boards, and gets his engine room bunk. | Calm harbor waters. Smells of diesel, bait, and old salt. |
| **5-7** | Leaving Barnegat Light, heading into the Mid-Atlantic shelf. First Galley Shift and the spilled stew. | **10-foot swells.** Rough initial transition. Coastal chop makes working extremely difficult for a greenhorn. |
| **8-14** | Offshore Mid-Atlantic. The Fish Hold Reveal and subsequent Contract Enforcement (Service). | Average offshore conditions: **2 to 5 ft seas.** The focus is internal (the freezing fish hold). |
| **15-16** | Open ocean, continuous fishing operations. Days blur as Leo craves the command. | Variable weather. Swells stabilize around **3 to 4 ft.** The physical grind sets in. |
| **17-19** | Heavy fishing zone. The "Money" Mishap and subsequent claiming in Leo's bunk. | **2 to 5 ft seas.** High-tension line operations require stable, but active waters. |
| **20** | **Belford, NJ.** Approaching harbor for offload. | Calming coastal waters, busy harbor traffic. |

## Book 2 Breakdown: The Night Watch

**Duration:** A longer 10-day trip.
**Start Location:** Departing Belford, NJ.
**End Location:** Safe Return to Port (likely heading towards Point Pleasant, NJ).

| Chapter | Location & Narrative Focus | Sea Conditions & Environment |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **1-2** | Docked in Belford, then departing for deeper waters under First Mate's watch. | Coastal chop, smoothing out as they head offshore. |
| **3-8** | Deep water. Refrigeration failure and the grueling engine room encounters/discipline. | Standard offshore conditions: **2 to 5 ft seas.** The oppressive heat of the engine room contrasts with the cool ocean air. |
| **9-12** | Deep water, extended night watches. Midnight galley operations and the wheelhouse tryst. | Calm but isolating. **2 to 4 ft seas.** Dark, radar-illuminated environment. |
| **13-17** | Mid-Atlantic, open ocean. The barometer drops and a massive storm system hits. | **Severe Storm Conditions:** High winds (20-30+ kt), dangerous, massive seas. Vessel is violently pitched. Survival mode. |
| **18-19** | The Eye of the Storm / immediate aftermath. Adrenaline-fueled claiming. | **Rough but stabilizing.** Swells dropping from storm heights to angry **6 to 8 ft chop.** |
| **20** | Limping back towards the coast. | Storm fully breaks. Swells recede back to **3 to 5 ft.** Leo accepts his place on the crew. |

## Book 3 Breakdown: The Captain's Catch

**Duration:** A long expedition spanning "weeks".
**Start Location:** Mid-Atlantic coast (e.g., Point Pleasant, NJ).
**End Location:** Final Disembarkation Port (Point Pleasant or Atlantic City, NJ).

| Chapter | Location & Narrative Focus | Sea Conditions & Environment |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **1-8** | Heading out for the long expedition. The Captain's observation, invitation, and the deep water claim. | Stable deep-water operations. **2 to 5 ft swells.** |
| **9-14** | Deep water Mid-Atlantic Bight / offshore canyons. Weeks pass. The biological shift, confession, and gestation worship. | Typical offshore weather cycles. Periods of calm (**2 to 3 ft seas**) and moderate chop. |
| **15-17** | Deep offshore. Mechanical failure and Leo proving his worth in the galley. | **2 to 5 ft seas.** The danger isn't the weather, but the isolation and drifting without power. |
| **18-19** | The Ultimate Sanctuary in the Captain's quarters. | Stable, calming seas as they begin the return journey. |
| **20** | **Point Pleasant / Atlantic City, NJ.** Harbor approach and docking. | Calming waters. The return to civilization marks the end of the journey and Leo's permanent integration into the crew. |
